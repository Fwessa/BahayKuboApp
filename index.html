ABOUT THE PROJECT

BAHAY KUBO: A 3D Mobile Simulation for TLE-HE Cookery is an innovative digital learning tool developed for Grade 9 students of Ramon Magsaysay High School, España. This project aims to transform traditional cookery laboratory activities into an interactive, engaging, and accessible experience through 3D simulation technology.

By leveraging mobile devices and immersive environments, Bahay Kubo allows students to explore a virtual kitchen, perform cooking procedures, and practice food preparation techniques in a safe and controlled setting. It supports competency-based learning and reinforces key TLE-HE (Cookery) concepts aligned with the K-12 curriculum.

This simulation not only enhances practical skills but also caters to students with limited access to physical laboratories or cooking materials. The project promotes inclusivity, creativity, and experiential learning, helping students build confidence and mastery in cooking tasks without the risk of real-world mishaps.

Why a Cooking Simulation Matters

With limited access to school kitchens or ingredients, many students miss out on hands-on experience. This simulation ensures every learner can practice cooking skills virtually, making learning safe, accessible, and repeatable anytime.
The simulation aims to develop fundamental cookery skills, enhance students’ understanding of food safety and hygiene, and foster critical thinking through practical problem-solving. It supports curriculum standards and encourages self-paced, experiential learning.
Students can build and refine essential cooking techniques such as measuring ingredients, following recipes, and mastering cooking methods — all within an interactive, risk-free environment.
Interactive features and realistic kitchen scenarios keep learners engaged, making lessons more immersive and enjoyable. By allowing students to explore, experiment, and repeat tasks at their own pace, the simulation promotes deeper understanding and long-term skill retention.
The simulation is designed to be inclusive, offering user-friendly controls and clear visual instructions to cater to various learning styles. Its mobile accessibility ensures that students can participate regardless of location or access to traditional kitchen facilities.

What Makes Bahay Kubo Different

Unlike typical learning apps, Bahay Kubo doesn’t rely on an internet connection or passive reading. It puts students in control of a virtual cooking space—letting them learn by doing. Each simulation is structured to mimic real-life cookery classes, complete with interactive tools, multimedia instructions, and offline access for uninterrupted learning.

Who Can Use It?

This simulation can be used by Grade 9 TLE-HE (Cookery) students who want to practice and enhance their cooking skills in a safe, virtual environment. It is also helpful for TLE teachers, as it provides an engaging tool to demonstrate lessons, assign interactive activities, and assess student performance. Additionally, schools with limited kitchen facilities or equipment can use this simulation as an alternative way to deliver practical cooking lessons without needing a full laboratory setup.

Students

Grade 9 TLE-HE (Cookery) students can use the simulation to safely practice cooking procedures and develop basic cookery skills, even without access to a real kitchen.

Teachers

TLE teachers can use the simulation as a teaching aid to visually demonstrate cooking lessons, give virtual practice tasks, and assess student understanding more effectively.
